Welcome To Ropes 2017

Welcome to the Muskoka Woods 2017 ropes team! It's great to have all of you guys on board and it's set to be an exciting Summer this year. I can't wait to meet all of you guys in person and work alongside you throughout the season! Muskoka Woods is an incredible place to spend your Summer. If you've worked here before, welcome home. If this is your first time working here you're about to discover something really special. I hope this season that we all get to know each other better and create an awesome community of instructors and a tight team.

About Me

My name is Tim Davidson and I'm your area head this season! I was born and raised in Northern Ireland, but also lived in England for eight years and Canada for one. I currently study Interaction Design at Ulster University. Basically it's graphic and web design (in case you hadn't already guessed) with a little bit of magic. When I'm not working in Muskoka Woods I work in NI's only 5 Star hotel, it's pretty sweet, I also do some freelance graphic design. Outside of work I really enjoy listening to music, watching TV shows and movies, hanging out with people, and water sports like sailing and kayaking.

This is my third Summer at Muskoka Woods but my fifth season. Last year I had the opportunity to work on site as an instructor for the year. During that time I got my Ropes Level 1 and did a lot of instruction before joining the Summer high ropes staff, so I got to play about on the course a lot. It's a cool job and there are so many different things you can do with ropes, there are always new challenges to try and different routes to explore. No two climbs are ever the same

The Team

This Summer's team is made up of 10 instructors and myself. Everyone has been chosen carefully because of their abilities and their genuine personalities. If you're feeling nervous before coming up to camp, don't be, you'll fit right in. Just remember, Muskoka Woods chose you over many other applicants and you're here for a reason! If you think you haven't got all the skills you think you'll need to be succesful, our team beleive you do, and if there is something missing, that's what staff weeks for. On top of that week, you guys will be going through ropes training to gain your Level 1. This is honestly such a great time to become a team and get to know each other over late nights reading green binders and tying endless amounts of knots in rope that's almost too short to tie knots in! This is also an awesome opportunity for you guys to get a qualification that can be used in a job when returning home after camp, your Level 1 qualifies you for a year, so make the most of it, quit school and go work at Treetop Trekking for a year!

I can't wait to see everyone come together and bring all their differences to the ropes shed. Bring something unique to you, something to help others and to bring a real positive vibe to the whole team and Muskoka Woods community! You'd be surprsised at the people you meet, the friends you leave with and the things you learn whether that be a new climbing technique or which drink combinations are the best in the dining halls (if you mix them all, it's something quite special)!

Apparel

Every year, each section produces their own apparel that they can wear during their time at camp and then forevermore. The way this works is everyone pays for their own, I'm trying to keep the cost to a minimum. This year the plan is to get t-shirts. The design, front and back, is shown below. Hopefully everyone is down to get one. It would be great to see everyone together wearing them. The plan is to get them ordered ASAP so we can wear them all season. In past years, clothing is ordered super late and is only seen on camp for a week! My goal is to have them for staff week, so if I can get a response from everyone before then, heads will be turning during staff week!

Awards

Over a career of more than 4 decades, Paula Scher has won many different awards. Here are a selection of the awards she has won.

Type Directors Club Medal

This is awarded to individuals that have made significant advances for, contributions to, and achievements in the art and craft of type and typography.

Chrysler Design Award

Achievements of individuals in innovative works of architecture and design which influenced modern American culture.

Other awards include:-

Art Directors Club Hall of Fame

Medallist, American Institute of Graphic Arts

Doctor of Fine Arts Honoris Causa, Corcoran College of Art and Design

2001 AIGA Medal: Distinction in identity design, packaging design, publication design, environmental graphics

“The goal of design is to raise the expectation of what design can be”

Paula Scher has contributed greatly to the advancement of graphic design through her revival of typefaces and historical designs, as well as creating new styles based on Art Deco and Russian Constructivism. She has inspired many artists and her work will continue to do so for many years to come. Her work and passion has undoubtedly raised the expectation and standards for all designers and artists across the world.
